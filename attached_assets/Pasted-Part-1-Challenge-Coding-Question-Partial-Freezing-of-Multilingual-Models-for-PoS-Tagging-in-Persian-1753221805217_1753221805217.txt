Part 1: Challenge Coding Question
Partial Freezing of Multilingual Models for PoS Tagging in Persian
Objective:
Explore the impact of partial layer freezing when fine-tuning a distilled multilingual transformer model for Part-of-Speech (PoS) tagging in Persian.
Background
Recent advances in deep learning suggest that partially freezing layers in pre-trained models can improve efficiency and preserve learned linguistic features, especially in tasks like PoS tagging. This challenge explores this concept using a distilled multilingual transformer model (such as distilmBERT) applied to Persian.
You are expected to have a background in:

Deep Learning (with PyTorch, Transformers, and HuggingFace)
Natural Language Processing (especially Part-of-Speech tagging)
If you haven't taken these courses, you're allowed extra time. Please complete as much as you can. Even a partial solution or preliminary experimentation is valuable.
Dataset
Use a subset of the Persian data from the Universal Dependencies (UD) dataset — for example, UD_Persian-Seraji. Choose a manageable size to allow faster training and iteration.
Tasks
Task 1: Dataset Preparation & Baseline Model
Load and preprocess the Persian UD dataset for PoS tagging. Use a tokenizer compatible with your model (e.g., BertTokenizerFast for distilmBERT).
Fine-tune a distilled multilingual model (e.g., distilmBERT) on the dataset to establish a baseline performance.
Use standard hyperparameters (e.g., batch size, learning rate) to reduce complexity and speed up training.
Task 2: Model Adjustment with Partial Freezing
Create a new instance of the same model and selectively freeze some of its layers based on your understanding of the architecture.
Fine-tune the partially frozen model on the same dataset. Only update the unfrozen layers during training.
Task 3: Evaluation and Analysis
Compare the baseline and partially frozen models using accuracy as the primary metric.
Reflect on your choices:
Why did you freeze those specific layers?
What impact did that have on performance?
How did the results compare to expectations?
Deliverables
A brief report summarizing your:
Dataset setup
Model configurations
Results and accuracy scores
Analysis of the impact of partial freezing
Time taken to complete the task (in hours)
Your code, preferably in a Jupyter Notebook, Colab, or a similar interactive format, to make it easy to test and reproduce.
Guidance & Tips
Layer Selection: Think about what early, middle, and late layers capture in BERT-based models and how freezing affects learning.
Resource Management: Use a small enough dataset subset to allow experimentation, but large enough to show performance trends.
Explain Your Choices: Your reasoning behind freezing decisions and dataset design is just as important as the results.
